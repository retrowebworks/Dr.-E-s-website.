Genetic & Epigenetic Susceptibility

Temporal Frame: Pre-conception / Early Embryonic
SCIENTIFIC MECHANISM ANALYSIS:

Initial biological vulnerability begins at the genetic and epigenetic level. Key polymorphisms in detoxification pathways (MTHFR, GST, SOD) create individual variation in the capacity to process environmental toxicants. Epigenetic modifications — methylation patterns established during gametogenesis — set the stage for how efficiently neural progenitor cells will respond to subsequent environmental challenges.

This phase represents the foundational "loading capacity" of the biological system. It does not predetermine outcome, but establishes the parameters within which all subsequent environmental interactions will operate.
